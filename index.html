ZMAP: Cross-study integration of zebrafish embryonic single-cell atlases provides consensus hierarchical views of cell type identity

ZMAP (Zebrafish Multi-study Atlas Project) is a harmonized single-cell RNA-seq reference atlas for zebrafish embryogenesis developed by the Wagner Lab at the University of California, San Francisco.

Nicole A. Aponte-Santiago1,2, Yingxin Su1,2, Nicole Suren1,2, and Daniel E. Wagner1,2*

1 Department of Obstetrics, Gynecology and Reproductive Science, Center for Reproductive Sciences, University of California San Francisco, San Francisco, CA, USA
2 Eli and Edythe Broad Center for Regeneration Medicine and Stem Cell Research, University of California San Francisco, San Francisco, CA, USA

* Correspondence: daniel.wagner@ucsf.edu

Abstract

Recent scRNA-seq studies have produced extensive maps of zebrafish embryogenesis, yet meta-study level comparisons remain challenging due to differences in sampling, processing, and annotation conventions. We present ZMAP (Zebrafish Multi-study Atlas Project), a harmonized reference integrating eight published datasets comprising over 790,000 cells across 15 developmental time windows from gastrulation through larval stages. ZMAP provides a shared embedding and a curated hierarchical annotation ontology spanning germ layer, tissue, cell type, and high-resolution clusters, enabling comparative analysis of cell identities across studies. We utilized ZMAP to derived consensus identity marker gene sets that reproducibly distinguish each ontology group, as well as to identify broadly expressed candidate housekeeping genes. Finally, we provide a web portal (https://wagnerlabucsf.github.io/zmap/) that supports interactive 2D and 3D exploration of the UMAP embedding, gene and annotation-level queries, and access to consensus marker resources. ZMAP thus serves as a unified reference for cross-study analysis, marker discovery, and annotation of new zebrafish scRNA-seq datasets.
